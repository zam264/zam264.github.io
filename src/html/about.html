About me

General

My name is Steven Raymond Zamborsky. I'm a 20'ish year old guy with a lot of different hobbies, interests and curiosities. I live in Western Pennsylvania, roughly 30 minutes from downtown Pittsburgh. I have had a passion for programming and development since my first computer science class in high school. I graduated Slippery Rock University of Pennsylvania in December of 2015 with a Computer Science degree and Information Technology minor. In college I practiced basic programming principles using Java and C++. I elected to take courses teaching the basics of web development using JavaScript. Additionally, I took classes for scripting in both Bash and Windows Command Prompt. Finally, classes such as Assembly and Discrete computing helped contribute to my appreciation and understanding of the field.

Work

From May of 2015 through February of 2018 I worked at a company called Creehan & Company as a Build and Release Engineer. Creehan creates workflow software for the specialty pharmacy industry. Working at Creehan I've learned a wealth of information and also developed my passion for automation. I helped develop a Continuous Integration and Continuous Delivery infrastructure. The CI/CD infrastructure helped make the software development lifecycle more dependable and consistent.

In February of 2018 I resigned from my position at Creehan & Company to pursue a Build and Deployment Engineer position at Duquesne Fund Services. I am a DevOps resource for them, primarily working with CI/CD pipelines and general automation of the SDLC. DFS provides technology solutions for hedge funds, asset managers and family offices.
